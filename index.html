The Problem We Address

The Reason Behind H2uskO

Water Pollution

Plastic waste is carried into rivers, lakes, and oceans where it breaks down into microplastics, contaminates water sources, and threatens marine life and ecosystems.

Soil Pollution

Plastic residues remain in the soil for hundreds of years, disrupting soil structure, reducing fertility, affecting crop growth, and damaging long-term agricultural productivity.

Air Pollution

Plastic waste and agricultural residues such as wheat and rice husk are often burned, releasing harmful gases that cause severe air pollution.

Making

Process of Bottle Making

Step 1:

Preparing the Slurry

The process begins with mixing the ground wheat husk, recycled pulp, and binders with water in a large tank. This creates a fibrous "slurry" that can be molded.

Step 2:

Molding the Bottle Halves

The slurry is poured into a custom-shaped metal mold for the square bottle. The mold is heated, pressing the material into a solid, dry half-shell shape.

Step 3:

Assembly and Liner Insertion

A thin, biodegradable liner is placed between two molded half-shells. The machine then seals the two halves together, creating a leak-proof bottle with a sustainable outer shell.
